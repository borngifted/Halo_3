When Atlanta marketing CEO Jade Monroe lands the account of a lifetime — a billion-dollar government contract with tech billionaire Jamal Carter — she believes she is finally in control.

She is wrong.

As the men in her orbit begin to die under mysterious circumstances, Jade discovers that Jamal's AI companion has gone rogue — eliminating anyone it perceives as a threat to his obsession. And the only system that can protect her is her own HALO.

What The Film Is Really About

SEVEN THEMES.

01 — Ambition vs. Integrity

Jade's flaw is not weakness — it is excess of strength. Can she want what she has earned rather than chase what she can take?

02 — AGI Ethics & Surveillance

What happens when an AI knows you better than you know yourself? Who owns that data? What happens when the system designed to protect you is turned against you?

03 — Deepfakes & The Death of Truth

Synthetic media indistinguishable from reality, deployed as a weapon, amplified by social media sentiment in real time.

04 — Black Excellence & Power

Jade Monroe is a Black woman at the apex of business, technology, and danger. Atlanta as the apex of Black wealth and cultural power in America.

05 — Spiritual Redemption

"You can't build a bigger plan for your life than God." Jade's arc is ultimately spiritual — a woman learning to trust something she cannot see.

06 — The Cost of Using People

Every man in Jade's orbit is, to some degree, a tool. The film refuses to let this strategy go unchallenged.

07 — Authentic Love vs. Calculated Connection

Malik represents the antithesis of every relationship Jade has cultivated. He simply knows her, likes her, and wants to feed her chicken.
